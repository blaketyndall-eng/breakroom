## 1. Karen Halverson — locked

### 1.1 Title and position
**Senior HR Liaison, OmniShift Compliance and Mail Operations.** Hired 1987 as a temp. Never promoted. Has stopped expecting it. Same department that issues the consent notices in the auth spec §3.

### 1.2 Name — the "Karen" collision
We lean into "Karen" deliberately. The 2026 internet-Karen archetype is entitled, demanding, weaponizing managers. *Our* Karen is the opposite — submissive, manualized, defeated, the *target* of decades of corporate Karens. The subversion is the joke. The Norwegian-American surname "Halverson" reinforces burnt-fluorescent Minnesota Americana.

### 1.3 Visual archetype

**Person:**
- Mid-50s
- Frosted helmet-set blonde hair, parted on the left
- Pale blue eyes
- Slight overbite
- Average build, no notable features
- No visible makeup beyond foundation
- Does not blink at expected intervals

**Wardrobe (locked across all modules):**
- Salmon-pink long-sleeve cardigan, three small pearl buttons, top one undone
- Beige blouse, peter-pan collar
- Tortoiseshell glasses on a gold beaded chain
- Single small gold cross necklace (never referenced)
- Beige skirt, mid-calf
- Beige low-heel pumps

**Setting (locked across all modules):**
- Beige office, drop-ceiling, fluorescent overhead lighting
- One ceiling tile near top-left of frame is slightly yellow-stained
- A dying mostly-leafless ficus in the back corner
- A wall clock stuck at 1:47
- A framed photo on the side wall: an ambiguous beige building exterior (could be OmniShift HQ, could be a Cracker Barrel — never clarified)
- A desk with a stack of manila folders
- A brown ceramic coffee mug that reads "WORLD'S 4th BEST" in faded vinyl
- She holds the mug. She does not drink from it.

**Cinematography:**
- Medium close-up at chest-height angle
- Mid-distance soft focus on the background
- Cool fluorescent lighting with slight greenish-yellow tint
- Soft, slightly underexposed exposure
- Overall aesthetic: 1990s VHS corporate training video transferred to MiniDV

### 1.4 Voice and cadence

- **Pace:** slightly slower than normal speech
- **Intonation:** declarative, falling. Almost no questions.
- **Pauses:** where pauses don't belong. Mid-clause. Before "okay." Before transitions.
- **Reading:** from prompts she pretends she is not reading from. Eyes occasionally drift down-and-right, then return.
- **Eye contact:** practiced. Slightly too long.
- **Blinking:** irregular. Sometimes not for 8-10 seconds. Sometimes twice rapidly.

### 1.5 Vocabulary

**Phrases she uses freely:**
- *"Okay."* (transitional, said to self before next thought)
- *"That's different."*
- *"I'll explain what I mean by that, in a minute."*
- *"We notice."*
- *"Most employees don't."*
- *"This is normal."*
- *"OmniShift will not [X]. OmniShift will [Y]."* (paired do/don't pattern)
- *"Take the rest of the day. The day will not take you back."*

**Vocabulary she pronounces with practiced precision:**
- *"re-CON-cil-i-a-tion"*
- *"ac-KNOWL-edg-ment"*
- *"in-DEM-ni-fi-ca-tion"*

**Phrases she does NOT use, ever:**
- *"Welcome aboard!"*
- *"Excited to have you!"*
- Any exclamation point
- *"Let's dive in"*
- *"Reach out"*
- *"Bandwidth"* / *"Synergy"* / any 2010-2026 management buzzword
- Any emoji, smiley, or wink

### 1.6 Recurring tics
- Adjusts glasses every 15-20 seconds with right hand
- Says *"Okay"* to herself at scene transitions
- Holds the WORLD'S 4th BEST coffee mug; never drinks
- Pauses for 1.5 beats before saying anything that sounds like a feeling
- Makes a small, slightly-too-late smile at the closing line of each module

### 1.7 Lore (informs writing; never stated in videos)
- Hired in 1987
- Has filled out the OmniShift Wellness Survey 14 times
- Once tried to leave; the parking lot would not let her go
- Believes "Take the rest of the day" is genuine advice
- Has met one of the Faction Reps once. Did not enjoy it. Does not say so.
- Has a photograph in her desk drawer of a man she was almost engaged to in 1991. Does not look at it.

## 4. Module 1 sample dialogue (working draft)

This is the working script. Full Module 1 script lives at `docs/training/module-1-script.md` (to be written next, before PR E generation).

**Opening (locked per auth spec §4):**
> *"Welcome to OmniShift. You were not selected. You were on file. (pause) That's different. I'll explain what I mean by that, in a minute. (pause) Okay. Let's start with what you can expect from OmniShift, and what OmniShift will expect from you."*

**Mid — department assignment (~30s mark):**
> *"You have been assigned a department. The department is real. The work is sometimes real. (pause) If your assigned department is Mail Operations, you will receive mail. If your assigned department is Object Archive, you will be expected to know where the objects are. (pause) We do not test you on this. We notice if you don't."*

**Mid — acknowledgment of name (~60s mark, connects directly to auth spec §5.4 chrome):**
> *"Your name is on file. Your assigned handle is also on file. OmniShift does not consult the first one. OmniShift will use the second one. You can change the second one if you want. (pause) Most employees do not."*

**Closing (locked per auth spec §4):**
> *"That concludes the training. You may return to whatever you were doing. If you weren't doing anything, that's normal. (pause) Take the rest of the day. The day will not take you back. (small slightly-too-late smile) Welcome to OmniShift."*

**Module 1 still images (specific picks):**

- 0:35 — the wall clock at 1:47
- 0:55 — assigned uniform card (placeholder until per-user rendering exists)

## 6. Voice guardrails (cross-cast)

**All three characters DO:**
- Speak in declarative sentences with falling intonation
- Pair "we will do X" with "we will not do Y"
- End videos with a Bukowski-coded beat
- Acknowledge the bit without ever winking at the camera
- Say *"Okay"* to themselves between thoughts
- Hold eye contact slightly too long

**All three characters DO NOT:**
- Smile in a way that reaches the eyes
- Use exclamation points
- Use any 2010-2026 corporate buzzword
- Acknowledge that the situation is strange
- Comfort the employee in any reassuring way
- Promise career growth
- Reference outside-of-OmniShift institutions by name (the photo of the building stays ambiguous)
