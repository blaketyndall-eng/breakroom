# The Breakroom Style Guide

## Visual Aesthetic

The Breakroom universe blends **lo‑fi baroque street surrealism** with early‑web nostalgia.  Our images and UI elements should never feel polished or sterile; they must carry the patina of age and the grime of real usage.  Key aesthetic principles include:

- **Luxury in decay:**  Our scenes juxtapose opulent objects (gold chains, fur coats, title belts) with cracked surfaces, pawn‑shop counters, and motel carpets.  Expensive things should look slightly out of place – sitting on a rusty table or hanging in a broken frame.
- **Sacred meets criminal:**  Crosses, saints, prayer candles, and Last Supper paintings sit beside cash rolls, bullets, boxing gloves, and uncashed checks.  This tension is central to the tone.
- **Cluttered shrines:**  Composition should feel curated like an altar, but messy like a workshop.  Layered objects, taped notes, cigarette burns, receipts, and fingerprints all belong.  Leave negative space for breathing room but avoid empty sterility.
- **Lo‑fi photographic texture:**  Use film grain, MiniDV compression artifacts, CCTV blur, VHS halation, and subtle vignette effects.  Shots should look like stills from an analog video rather than high‑res product photography.
- **Mixed lighting:**  No single light source.  Combine fluorescent spill (green), sodium‑vapor street glow (amber), candlelight (orange), TV blue, and dashboard LEDs.  Let shadows swallow corners.
- **Old‑web UI:**  For web pages (SleeperNet, Rack, tournaments), lean on 2000‑era layouts: table borders, blue hyperlinks, green header bars, visitor counters, and under‑lined navigation.  Incorporate small icons (floppy disk, printer, GIF badges) sparingly as nostalgic accents.

## Graphic Elements

- **Icons:**  Only use icons sparingly and never on title slides.  Avoid modern flat icons; instead, choose small pixel icons or low‑res images when needed (e.g., printer icon next to “Printable Version”).
- **Backgrounds:**  Use textures like aged paper, motel carpets, scuffed metal, and stained upholstery.  Avoid gradients or pure flat fills.
- **Images:**  When necessary, source photographs of real‑world objects (not generated) such as motel keys, fuzzy dice, and neon signs.  Use image generation only for abstract textures, never for identifiable objects, logos, or text.  Apply cropping or contain sizing appropriately.
- **Color palette:**
  - **Ash Black (#12110f)** for dark backgrounds and typography.
  - **Nicotine Beige (#d8c9a6)** for paper backgrounds and aged surfaces.
  - **Felt Green (#1f4a32)** for old table headers and pool‑table references.
  - **Dashboard Green (#38ff7a)** for UI highlights and signal indicators.
  - **Beer‑Sign Red (#b62b24)** for warnings, stamps, and error text.
  - **Motel Blue (#2c5f9e)** for link highlights and hotel references.
  - **Chrome Silver (#c7c7be)** for metallic accents.
  - **Hyperlink Blue (#0000ee)** for links in old‑web pages.
  - **Stamp Red (#b00020)** for red stamped overlays.

## Tone and Voice

- **Dry and plainspoken:**  All copy, from UI labels to lore descriptions, should be straightforward and blunt.  Avoid corporate jargon unless it is intentionally satirical (e.g., OmniShift memos).
- **Deadpan humour:**  Funny moments are delivered without wink; “You have been hired. Unfortunately.”
- **Human honesty:**  Rogue employees speak like tired coworkers who see through the company façade: “A bunch of bullsh*t, right?”
- **Narrative ambiguity:**  The site does not explain; it suggests.  The AI may have gone wrong, but nobody knows for sure.  Leave open questions for the user to explore.

## Content Structure

- **Three‑layer hierarchy:**  SleeperNet (public, corporate veneer), OmniShift (fake corporate back‑end), The Breakroom (underground human layer).  Users must peel back layers via actions like sign‑up and Clock Out.
- **Dual identities:**  Every user gets an **OmniShift Employee profile** (assigned by AI) and an **After Hours persona** (earned via Idle Hands registration).  Visual styles should differentiate the profiles (corporate vs. dive‑bar aesthetics).
- **Object lore:**  Place objects at the centre of pages.  Clicking a motel key reveals a file; saving fuzzy dice unlocks a secret.  Use object icons and labels consistently.
- **Secrets and rewards:**  Hide easter eggs behind actions (e.g., clicking the clock, saving combos of objects).  Unlock pages, phone messages, or items as rewards.
